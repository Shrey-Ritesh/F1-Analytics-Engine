### 1. Leakage audit on tire_degradation_rate
Answer:
- Yes, the formula used directly incorporates lap_time_seconds (specifically `df['lap_time_seconds'] - stint_min_time`). Because it literally subtracts the target base from the feature, it provided the model a massive, cheating shortcut effectively looking into the future.
- The leaky `tire_degradation_rate` has now been permanently excluded from the `train_lap_time_model.py` feature set.
- Output: The model was retrained, yielding a new honest RMSE of 8.14s on out-of-sample data.

### 4. Circuit Anomaly Investigation Findings
- **Canada Diagnosis (D):** Genuine Model Limitation. The model inherently expects slower baselines. Canada was unexpectedly faster in 2025 across all tires. 1.04x weather filters had negligible effect.
- **Britain Diagnosis (B):** Weather Contamination. 2024 Silverstone was an extreme wet race. Over 80% of laps were cut by the pipeline's wet-tire exclusion, leaving only 136 anomalous slicks surviving.
- Action: Added both (plus Australia, which failed on sample size) to a new `model_metadata.json` file acting as a "low-confidence" strategy simulation flag.

### 5. Strategy Simulation Known Limitations
Known limitations for strategy simulation:
- Mean residual: -1.05s (correct at inference by adding 1.05s per lap)
- Undercut precision: model resolves strategy deltas to ~2-3s accuracy
  Undercuts within 1s window should be treated as inconclusive
- Low confidence circuits: Canada, Britain, Australia (widen bounds ±4s)
